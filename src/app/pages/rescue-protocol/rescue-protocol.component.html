Rescue protocols

If you find — or lose — a pet

Practical steps for islanders and visitors: safety first, calm handling, next contacts. This isn’t veterinary or legal advice — use judgement and call professionals when unsure.

Finding a stray or injured animal

  1. Stay safe — traffic, frightened animals bite; don’t chase into roads.
  2. If possible, secure calmly (lead, towel, crate) without cornering aggressively. Offer water — not food unless a vet advises.
  3. Check for ID — collar tags, council registration where relevant, microchip at a vet.
  4. Post a sighting on this site with area, time, photo, collar colour — so owners can recognise their pet quickly.
  5. Found a native bird? See bird rescue on Island resources — do not keep protected wildlife without a DOC carer.

Lost your pet

  • Create a lost report here with photo, collar/microchip details, and last known bay or beach access.
  • Inform neighbours locally; ferry terminals and vets see many island pets.
  • Refresh water bowls at home quietly — some cats return overnight when it’s calm.

When to escalate

Severe injuries, aggression you can’t manage, or livestock at risk — involve local authorities or your vet as appropriate for your situation in New Zealand.
