How it works

One place for island pet alerts

Waiheke Pets Alert exists so lost animals and sightings don’t get buried in scattered group chats — neighbours can act quickly with clear, shared information.

Browse alerts

The main feed shows recent reports from around the island. Use the Status filter to narrow to Lost pet (someone is searching) or Sighted pet (someone has seen or is safely holding an animal). Search and species filters help you scan faster.

Open any card for full details, map context, and community notes from people nearby.

Report a pet

Tap Report Pet to publish a lost or sighted report. Add what you know — location, time, photos, collar or microchip hints — so others can recognise the animal without guesswork.

Clear reports get shared and acted on sooner; vague ones are easy to scroll past.

Why we built this

Waiheke’s geography and ferry links mean a missing pet can move between bays or off-island fast. A dedicated, island-wide alert board keeps everyone — full-time residents, weekenders, and visitors — on the same page.

The goal is simple: reunite pets with their people, and give good Samaritans an easy way to help without hunting through dozens of threads.
